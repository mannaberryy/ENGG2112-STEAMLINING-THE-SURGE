Financial efficiency

Reduced manual physiological scoring time and constrained over-escalation — Model 1 precision 0.882 limits unnecessary ICU referrals — contributes downstream efficiency gains in clinical time and critical care resource use with measurable cost implications under sustained surge.

Users and stakeholders
Direct users — Front-line triage clinicians
Input biomarker measurements. Receive severity classification, deterioration prediction, confidence scores, and priority categories to inform escalation decisions.
Operational stakeholders — Hospital administrators
Input real-time ICU capacity into the surge simulation. Receive allocation strategy recommendations calibrated to current resource constraints.
Institutional stakeholders — Governing health bodies
Interpret aggregate capacity and demand data from the system to inform critical care funding and policy decisions.
Affected population — Admitted patients
Not users of the tool. Outcomes — escalation decisions, ICU access, survival — are determined by the quality of triage decisions made through the framework.
Unintended consequences
Alert fatigue

64 false positive flags may condition clinicians to discount alerts, eroding clinical value over time.

Latency risk

Under-24hr biomarker window introduces delay in time-critical presentations where deterioration precedes results.

Deskilling

Progressive reliance on priority scores may erode triage capacity without algorithmic support.

MVP feasibility: AUROC 0.890 with 18 accessible biomarkers — cross-institutional deployment without full metabolomic infrastructure.
