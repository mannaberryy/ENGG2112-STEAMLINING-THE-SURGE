Users and stakeholders
Direct users — Front-line triage clinicians
Input biomarker measurements. Receive severity, deterioration, confidence scores, and priority category to inform escalation.
Operational stakeholders — Hospital administrators
Input real-time ICU capacity into surge simulation. Receive allocation strategy recommendations for their resource constraints.
Institutional stakeholders — Governing health bodies
Interpret aggregate capacity and demand data to inform critical care funding and policy decisions.
Affected population — Admitted patients
Not users of the tool. Escalation outcomes and survival are directly determined by the quality of triage decisions made through the framework.
Unintended consequences
Alert fatigue

64 false positive flags may condition clinicians to discount alerts, eroding tool value over time.

Latency risk

Under-24hr biomarker window introduces delay risk where deterioration precedes the result.

Deskilling

Progressive reliance on priority scores may erode triage capacity without algorithmic support.

Discussion · 9.3

Limitations and ethical considerations

Data, consent and legal
Informed consent — secondary data use Moulaei 2025
CBD and CLD collected for research. Clinical deployment and rolling retraining both constitute secondary use — requiring separate consent governance beyond treatment consent.
Patient transparency — no contestability Webb 2024
Patients receive a priority score determining ICU access with no mechanism to query it. Limited contestation leads operators to infer equitable functionality — reinforcing bias rather than identifying it. Binary matrix limits review granularity.
Legal accountability gap Basu 2020
No regulatory framework assigns liability for probabilistic ML outputs in clinical settings. Responsibility between institution, clinician, and developer is unresolved and unaddressed in the prototype.
Automation bias — clinician over-reliance Elhaddad 2024
Systematic dependence on algorithmic outputs may suppress situational expertise. Clinicians may adjust engagement to recommendations, de-prioritising independent contextual assessment.
Model performance, bias and equity
False negative patient safety risk Kiekkas 2021
61 deteriorating patients misclassified as stable — assigned lower escalation priority. Delayed ICU admission independently associated with 61% increase in mortality odds.
Demographic bias — limited generalisation
Model 1 trained on 43 Mexican patients (Delta + Omicron). Without cross-validation on non-Mexican populations, the CDSS risks biasing toward learned physiological profiles across diverse cohorts.
Socioeconomic equity — compounding marginalisation Juhn 2022
30% missingness threshold excludes under-resourced patients from training. At inference, same institutions use the weaker rapid-test variant — 78 vs 61 false negatives. Highest undetected deterioration risk attributed to patients with the least institutional protection.
Deployment readiness — prototype gaps
Section 8 does not describe encrypted data transport, access control, or audit logging for patient biomarker data. No EMR or laboratory system integration is implemented. Current state is a functional prototype, not a clinically deployable system.
